Overview

Disney animation of a classic tale. Beautiful princess Rapunzel has been locked away in a tower since she was captured as a baby by an old hag. Her magical long blonde hair has the power to provide eternal youth, and the evil Gothel uses this power to keep her young. At the age of 18, Rapunzel becomes curious about the outside world, and when a prince uses her tower as a refuge, she asks him to help her escape.

Pascal and Maximus are by far the greatest disney sidekicks Disney has created they don't talk but are just so perfectly hilarious. Rapunzel is a fearless princess with an amazing personality but Eugene "Flynn Ryder" Fitzherbert is by far the most logical, funniest disney male lead out there with Moana and maui, and Tiana and naveen coming in closely behind. I'd advise anyone with a sense of humor, who loves strong females, funny animals and a handsome prince and of course songs to watch this movie Immediately.

It has great story pacing and character chemistry that makes you care a lot about how Rapunzel and Eugenes relationship develop. The lantern scene and Eugenes death scene are some of the most beautiful I’ve ever seen in a movie. The humor is clever and creative and the songs greatly set the mood for their scenes. It does the opposite of many cliches of previous Disney movies. Eugene has subtle self awareness of Disney tropes that are part of the movies humor. None of the two lead characters fall in love until they learn more about each other throughout their journey. They don’t get married until years after the films events, as stated by Eugene at the end. The princess helps the man as much as he helps her. The female saves the male. The moral is about trying to fulfill dreams and that there’s always more dreams to have after it’s fulfilled, which is a very inspiring and more applicable message people can relate to than that of the later movies. It’s strange to me that Tangled isn’t talked about as much as the later Disney movies and that some people don’t think it’s as original. Tangled is my favorite movie.
